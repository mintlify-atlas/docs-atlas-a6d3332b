Creative! provides a comprehensive video playback system with intuitive timeline controls for precise navigation during gameplay analysis.

## Timeline Navigation

The video timeline allows you to navigate through your gameplay footage with precision. You can interact with the timeline in several ways:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click to Jump">
    Click anywhere on the timeline bar to instantly jump to that point in the video. The playback position updates immediately to match your click location.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Drag the Handle">
    Click and drag the timeline handle to scrub through the video. The video automatically pauses while you're dragging, allowing you to find the exact frame you need.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Jump to Breakpoints">
    Click on any breakpoint marker (shown as colored circles on the timeline) to instantly jump to that analysis point. This automatically selects the breakpoint and displays its associated annotations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The timeline calculates positions as percentages of the total video duration, supporting videos of any length without performance degradation.
</Note>

## Playback Controls

Creative! offers professional-grade playback controls for analyzing gameplay at different speeds and directions:

### Standard Controls

The control bar provides five essential buttons:

- **Skip to Start**: Instantly jump to the beginning of the video
- **Rewind**: Play the video in reverse
- **Play/Pause**: Toggle between playing and pausing
- **Fast Forward**: Play the video at increased speed
- **Skip to End**: Instantly jump to the end of the video

### Variable Speed Playback

Both rewind and fast forward support multiple speed levels:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Fast Forward Modes">
    Click the fast forward button multiple times to cycle through speed levels:
    - First click: Plays forward at increased speed
    - Subsequent clicks: Further increases playback speed
    - Click while already fast-forwarding: Cycles to next speed level ("loop" mode)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Rewind Modes">
    Click the rewind button multiple times to cycle through speed levels:
    - First click: Plays in reverse at increased speed
    - Subsequent clicks: Further increases reverse playback speed
    - Click while already rewinding: Cycles to next speed level ("loop" mode)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>
  When you activate playback controls (play, rewind, or fast forward), any selected breakpoint is automatically deselected to prevent conflicts between manual and breakpoint-based navigation.
</Info>

## Trim Mode

When you enter trim mode, the playback controls transform to show trim-specific options:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Trim Mode">
    Activate the trim tool from the toolbar. The standard playback controls are replaced with trim handles on the timeline.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Trim Points">
    Drag the start and end handles on the timeline to select the portion of video you want to keep. The handles show as percentages of the total video duration.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm or Cancel">
    Two buttons replace the playback controls:
    - **Cancel**: Exits trim mode without making changes
    - **Confirm**: Applies the trim and removes any breakpoints outside the selected range
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  When you confirm a trim operation, Creative! automatically cleans up any breakpoints that fall outside the new time range using `cleanupBreakpoints()`. This ensures your annotations remain synchronized with the trimmed video.
</Note>

## Timeline Visualization

The timeline provides visual feedback to help you understand your position in the video:

- **Progress Bar**: A colored bar fills from left to right showing playback progress
- **Smooth Transitions**: Progress updates use configurable transition timing (default 0.1s) for smooth animation
- **Instant Seeking**: When you manually seek, transitions are disabled (0s) for immediate response
- **Breakpoint Markers**: Colored circles appear at breakpoint positions on the timeline

<Tip>
  The timeline uses CSS custom properties (`--video-progress` and `--transition-time`) for smooth, hardware-accelerated animations that won't slow down even with long videos.
</Tip>

## Disabled State

The playback controls automatically disable in certain situations:

- When no video is loaded (cursor shows "not-allowed")
- When annotation tools are active (except trim mode)
- During certain editing operations

When disabled, a semi-transparent overlay appears over the controls to clearly indicate they're unavailable, and all control interactions are prevented.
