# Analyze your first gameplay video

Get up and running with Creative! by creating your first gameplay analysis in just a few minutes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch Creative!">
    Open the Creative! application on your computer. Make sure you have it installed — if not, check out the [installation guide](/installation) first.
    
    You'll see the main interface with a placeholder screen and disabled controls until you load a video.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Load a gameplay video">
    You can load a video in two ways:
    
    **Load a new video file:**
    - Click **Select Video** or drag and drop a video file
    - Supported formats: MP4, WebM, MOV
    - The video will appear in the playback area
    
    **Load an existing analysis:**
    - Click **Load Analysis File**
    - Select an `.an` file you previously exported
    - Your video, breakpoints, and all annotations will be restored
    
    <Info>Creative! supports large video files efficiently using streaming playback.</Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Switch to Analysis Mode">
    At the top of the window, you'll see two mode buttons:
    
    - **Replay Mode**: Simple video playback without editing tools
    - **Analysis Mode**: Full annotation and breakpoint creation tools
    
    Click **Analysis Mode** to enable the analysis tools. The interface will expand to show the analysis menu on the right side.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first breakpoint">
    Navigate to the moment in your video you want to analyze:
    
    1. Use the timeline to scrub to a specific time, or click anywhere on the timeline to jump
    2. Click the **+** button in the analysis menu to create a breakpoint at the current timestamp
    3. Your breakpoint appears on the timeline as a marker
    
    <Tip>Breakpoints are automatically created at the current playback position.</Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add annotations">
    With a breakpoint selected, choose an annotation tool:
    
    **Drawing tool:**
    - Click the **Draw** icon
    - Select a color (red, green, magenta, or yellow)
    - Draw directly on the video frame to highlight important areas
    
    **Text notes:**
    - Click the **Text** icon
    - Type your analysis and observations
    - Notes are draggable and resizable on the canvas
    
    **Voice notes:**
    - Click the **Voice** icon
    - Record audio commentary explaining your analysis
    - Playback your recordings directly in the app
    
    <CardGroup cols={3}>
      <Card title="Drawing" icon="pen">
        Highlight areas with colored annotations
      </Card>
      <Card title="Text" icon="note">
        Add written explanations
      </Card>
      <Card title="Voice" icon="microphone">
        Record audio commentary
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create more breakpoints">
    Repeat the process to add more analysis points throughout your video:
    
    1. Scrub to a new position
    2. Click **+** to create another breakpoint
    3. Add drawings, text, or voice notes
    
    You can navigate between breakpoints using the timeline markers or the Previous/Next buttons.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Trim your video (optional)">
    If you want to focus on a specific segment:
    
    1. Click the **Trim** button in the analysis menu
    2. Drag the trim handles on the timeline to set start and end points
    3. Click **Confirm Trim** when ready
    
    <Note>Trimming doesn't modify your original video file. The trim is only applied when you export the analysis.</Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export your analysis">
    Save your work as an analysis file:
    
    1. Click **Export Analysis File** in the analysis menu
    2. Choose a location and filename
    3. The app creates an `.an` file containing:
       - Your video (trimmed if applicable)
       - All breakpoints with timestamps
       - Drawings, text notes, and voice recordings
    
    <Info>Analysis files are ZIP archives that bundle everything together for easy sharing.</Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share or continue editing">
    Now you can:
    
    - Share your `.an` file with teammates, coaches, or friends
    - Load it later to continue editing
    - Create multiple versions with different analyses
    
    Recipients can open your `.an` file in Creative! to see your complete analysis with all annotations intact.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for effective analysis

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use descriptive breakpoints">
    Create breakpoints at key moments: successful plays, mistakes, decision points, or interesting strategies. This makes it easier to navigate your analysis later.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Combine annotation types">
    Use drawings to highlight visual elements, text notes for detailed explanations, and voice notes for longer commentary. Each tool serves a different purpose.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Export frequently">
    Save your work periodically by exporting analysis files. This creates backups and lets you share work-in-progress with others.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Organize your files">
    Use descriptive filenames like `valorant-clutch-round-analysis.an` or `cs-smoke-lineup-guide.an` to keep your analyses organized.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Understanding the modes

Creative! has two distinct modes:

| Mode | Purpose | Tools Available |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **Replay Mode** | Simple video playback and viewing | Timeline navigation, playback controls |
| **Analysis Mode** | Full editing and annotation | All replay tools + breakpoints, drawing, text, voice, trim |

Switch between modes anytime using the mode buttons at the top of the window. Your breakpoints and annotations are preserved when switching modes.
