This guide walks you through the complete process of creating a gameplay analysis in Creative!, from importing your video to exporting a finished analysis file.

## Importing Your Gameplay Video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch Creative!">
    Open the Creative! application on your system.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a video file">
    Click anywhere on the placeholder screen that says "Select a video file to replay!" to open the file browser.
    
    <Note>
    Creative! supports the following video formats:
    - MP4
    - WebM
    - MOV
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your file">
    Navigate to your gameplay recording and select it. The video will load automatically and playback controls will become available.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the Interface

Creative! has two distinct modes:

### Replay Mode
Use Replay Mode to watch your gameplay video without modifications. This mode is ideal for:
- Reviewing footage to identify key moments
- Watching your completed analysis
- General video playback

### Analysis Mode
Switch to Analysis Mode to create and edit your analysis. This mode enables:
- Creating breakpoints at specific timestamps
- Adding annotations (drawings, text, voice notes)
- Trimming the video
- Organizing your thoughts

<Info>
Click on "Analysis Mode" at the top of the screen to switch modes. The analysis menu will slide in from the right side.
</Info>

## Creating Breakpoints

Breakpoints are the foundation of your analysis. They mark specific moments in your gameplay where you want to add notes or observations.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the moment">
    Use the playback controls to find the exact timestamp you want to analyze. You can:
    - Click on the timeline to jump to a specific time
    - Use the skip to start/end buttons
    - Use rewind and fast forward controls
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pause the video">
    Click the play/pause button to pause at your desired moment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Switch to Analysis Mode">
    If you're not already in Analysis Mode, click "Analysis Mode" at the top of the screen.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create the breakpoint">
    A "Create Breakpoint" button will appear on the video. Click it to create a breakpoint at the current timestamp.
    
    <Note>
    Once created, the breakpoint is automatically selected and you can immediately start adding annotations.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding Annotations

Once you have a breakpoint selected, you can add different types of annotations to explain your analysis.

### Drawing Annotations

Draw directly on the video frame to highlight movement patterns, positioning, or strategies.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Draw tool">
    In the Analysis Menu on the right, click on "Draw" in the Tools section.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a color">
    When the Draw tool is active, a color picker appears showing four colors:
    - Red
    - Green
    - Magenta
    - Yellow
    
    Click your preferred color.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Draw on the video">
    Click and drag on the video to draw. Your annotations are saved automatically to the current breakpoint.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deselect when done">
    Click "Draw" again to exit drawing mode.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Text Notes

Add written explanations, observations, or theories about the gameplay moment.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Text Note tool">
    In the Analysis Menu, click on "Text Note" in the Tools section.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your text">
    A text input area will appear. Type your analysis, observations, or notes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your note">
    Your text is automatically saved to the breakpoint. Click "Text Note" again to exit text mode.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Voice Notes

Record audio commentary to explain your thoughts verbally.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Voice Note tool">
    In the Analysis Menu, click on "Voice Note" in the Tools section.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Record your commentary">
    Click the record button to start recording. Speak your analysis or observations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Stop recording">
    Click the stop button when finished. Your voice note is saved as a WAV file attached to the breakpoint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Make sure to grant microphone permissions when prompted, otherwise voice recording will not work.
</Warning>

## Trimming Your Video

If you only want to include a specific segment of your gameplay in the analysis, you can trim the video.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Trim Mode">
    In the Analysis Menu under the "Edit" section, click on "Trim".
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set trim points">
    Two handles will appear on the timeline:
    - Drag the left handle to set the start point
    - Drag the right handle to set the end point
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm or cancel">
    - Click "Confirm" to apply the trim
    - Click "Cancel" to discard changes
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
When you export, the video will be trimmed to your selected segment and all breakpoint timestamps will be adjusted accordingly.
</Info>

## Managing Breakpoints

### Navigating Between Breakpoints
- Click on any breakpoint marker on the timeline to select it
- The video will jump to that breakpoint's timestamp
- The Analysis Menu will display tools for the selected breakpoint

### Deleting a Breakpoint
<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the breakpoint">
    Click on the breakpoint you want to delete on the timeline.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Delete it">
    Click the "Delete Breakpoint" button at the bottom of the Analysis Menu.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Deleting a breakpoint removes all associated annotations (drawings, text, and voice notes). This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Create breakpoints strategically">
    Focus on key moments like:
    - Critical decision points
    - Mistakes or opportunities for improvement
    - Successful plays worth replicating
    - Patterns or strategies to remember
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use multiple annotation types">
    Combine drawings, text, and voice notes for comprehensive analysis:
    - Use drawings to highlight visual elements
    - Use text for concise observations
    - Use voice notes for detailed explanations or theories
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Organize chronologically">
    Create breakpoints in chronological order to make your analysis easier to follow when reviewing or sharing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Save regularly">
    Export your analysis periodically to avoid losing work (see [Exporting & Sharing](/guides/exporting-sharing)).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
